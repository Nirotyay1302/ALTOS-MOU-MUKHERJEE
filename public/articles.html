7 Impressive Ways Vitamin C Benefits Your Body

Vitamin C is one of the essential vitamins that our body needs, though our body is unable to produce it. Vitamin C is found in many types of Fruits & Vegetables which inculcate Amla, Oranges, Kiwi, Lemon, Broccoli & Spinach etc. On an average per day, an adult man should take 90 mg whereas an adult woman should take 75 mg of vitamin C. These days it is not possible to get recommended vitamin C through fruits & Vegetables, we need to take supplements to fulfill our daily needs.

Listed below are major health benefits of Vitamin C:

1. VITAMIN C & IMMUNITY

Healing of wounds is characterized by synthesis of connective tissue, the major component of which is collagen. Vitamin C helps the body produce collagen and is present in skin, muscle, and other tissues. People with a low intake of vitamin C may experience slower wound healing, as their bodies will be less able to produce collagen.

Daily Nutrition & Altos Supplements

Nutrition is the science that interprets the nutrients and other substances in food in relation to Maintenance, Growth, Reproduction, Health & Disease of a person. It includes ingestion, absorption, assimilation, biosynthesis, catabolism and excretion.

The diet of an organism is what it eats, which is largely determined by the availability and palatability of foods. For humans, a healthy diet includes preparation of food and storage methods that preserve nutrients from oxidation, heat or leaching and that reduces risk of foodborne illnesses. The seven major classes of human nutrients are Carbohydrates, Fats, Fiber, Minerals, Proteins, Vitamins & Water. Nutrients can be grouped as either macronutrients or micronutrients (needed in small quantities).

In humans an unhealthy diet can cause deficiency such as blindness, anaemia, scurvy, preterm birth, stillbirth and cretinism, or nutrient excess health-threatening conditions such as obesity and metabolic syndrome and such common chronic systematic diseases as cardiovascular diseases, diabetes and osteoporosis. Under nutrition can lead to wasting in acute cases, and stunting due to marasmus in chronic cases of malnutrition.

Effective Treatment For Irregular Periods

Menstrual irregularity is a highly common concern faced by women today. It is often driven by physical and emotional stress, irregular sleep patterns, hormonal imbalances, and nutritional deficiencies. While temporary chemical solutions are available, Ayurveda offers a safe, side-effect-free, and permanent treatment to regulate the menstrual cycle and restore complete uterine health.

The Ayurvedic Perspective

In Ayurveda, menstrual irregularities are primarily caused by an imbalance in the Vata dosha (specifically Apana Vata, which governs reproductive functions). When Vata is aggravated by stress, lack of sleep, or dry/processed food, it disrupts the flow of the cycle, leading to delayed periods, severe pain, or scanty bleeding.

Effective Natural Treatments:

1. Dietary Adjustments

Incorporate iron-rich foods, fresh leafy vegetables, sesame seeds, almonds, and warm jaggery into your diet. Avoid excessive intake of cold drinks, fast food, and highly spicy/dry foods, as they aggravate Vata dosha.

2. Daily Yoga and Relaxation

Gentle pelvic-opening yoga postures like Baddha Konasana (Butterfly Pose), Bhujangasana (Cobra Pose), and Malasana (Garland Pose) stimulate blood circulation to the pelvic area and help regulate ovarian function. Practicing Pranayama (breathing exercises) helps reduce stress-induced cortisol, a major trigger for skipped periods.

3. Uterine Tonics (Ashoka, Lodhra & Shatavari)

Herbs like Ashoka (Saraca asoca) and Lodhra (Symplocos racemosa) have long been revered as natural uterine tonics that stop abnormal bleeding, soothe spasms, and strengthen the uterine muscles. Shatavari acts as a natural adaptogen that balances female hormones.

"Nourishing the female reproductive system with time-tested herbs is key to restoring hormonal balance without chemicals."

Improve Your Digestive Health

In Ayurveda, good health begins in the gut. All wellness and vitality depend on the strength of your digestive fire (Agni). If Agni is weak, even healthy food cannot be digested properly, leading to the accumulation of toxic waste (Ama) in the gut. Over time, Ama enters the bloodstream, giving rise to lethargy, bloating, acid reflux, skin breakouts, and joint pain.

Optimizing your digestive health is the fastest way to increase energy, enhance nutrient absorption, and improve immunity. Here are four effective guidelines to restore your digestive fire:

1. Practice Mindful Eating

Avoid eating when distracted by phones, TV, or intense work. Chew your food thoroughly (about 30 times per bite) to ensure it mixes with salivary enzymes. This kickstarts digestion, significantly easing the stomach's workload.

2. Sip Warm Water and Avoid Ice

Drinking ice-cold water during or after meals dampens the digestive fire (Agni) like pouring cold water on a burning flame. Instead, sip warm water or ginger tea during meals. This stimulates gastric enzymes and aids smooth bowel movements.

3. Maintain Regular Eating Slots

Try to eat your meals at the same time every day. This schedules your internal biological clock to secrete digestive juices predictably, preventing acid build-up, gas, and bloating.

"When digestion is correct, medicine is of no need. When digestion is incorrect, medicine is of no use." — Ayurvedic Proverb

4. Incorporate Herbs and Digestive Syrups

Time-tested carminative herbs like Haritaki, Ajwain, Fennel, and Aloe Vera stimulate stomach acids and soothen the intestinal lining.

Tulsi is rich in phytochemicals, flavonoids, and essential oils that possess strong antiviral, antibacterial, and antifungal properties. Daily intake helps stimulate the activity of natural killer (NK) cells and T-helper cells, boosting your body's resistance to seasonal bugs.

The compounds in Tulsi (such as eugenol, cineole, and camphene) act as natural expectorants. They help clear congestion in the lungs, soothe sore throats, and provide instant relief from asthma, dry coughs, bronchitis, and common colds.

Tulsi is a powerful natural adaptogen, meaning it helps the body adapt to physical, chemical, and emotional stress. Consuming Tulsi tea or concentrated drops helps regulate cortisol (the stress hormone), calming the central nervous system and promoting mental peace.

Tulsi contains antioxidants that combat oxidative stress, helping to lower blood pressure and protect blood vessels from cholesterol damage. Some clinical trials also suggest it helps regulate blood glucose levels.
